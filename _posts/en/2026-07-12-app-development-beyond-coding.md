As I mentioned in the first entry in this development journey, I am a developer.

I am used to building the features I want, thinking about better performance, and writing clean, robust code that is easy to maintain. With Claude Code and Codex, I had fun creating two Android apps and a Windows program.

As a developer, I would usually have been satisfied at that point. If I put in a little more effort, I might write a friendly user manual or introduce a new feature at a seminar or user meeting. That was about it.

## The Other World I Found After Development

But telling other people about the self-sufficient apps I had built for my own needs was an entirely different world.

A month or two later, my expectation that I would quickly attract hundreds or thousands of users had completely disappeared. Instead, I found myself wrestling with questions like these:

- How can I get these apps in front of the public?
- Who actually needs them?
- How should I market and promote them from a business perspective?

Following AI's suggestions, I made AI-generated short videos and shared them as Instagram Reels. I am now comfortable creating short-form videos with GPT Image and Google Flow and editing them in CapCut.

Even so, the user base did not grow easily. Because I was targeting the Korean market first, I opened a Naver Blog and started writing. After all, when I need to find something in Korea, I still search Naver or browse Naver Cafes myself.

## Promotion Was Another Series of Trials and Errors

I offer every app and program I have built for free because I still do not have a clear business model, but users have not come easily. I also created a Threads account as another way to promote them.

Threads, however, was extremely sensitive to activity that appeared to be advertising or AI-driven. In the end, my account was permanently suspended twice. I was stopped while trying to grow my follower count before I had even managed to promote anything properly. I wrote about that experience in an earlier post.

## Rebuilding My GitHub Pages Site for Global Search

Naver Blog may perform well in Naver Search, but the real market for my apps and services is global. When I search Google for apps like mine, some of them do not appear at all.

That is why I am using Codex to update my existing GitHub Pages site, which originally served as both my developer page and app showcase.

First, I asked Codex to recommend three representative sites that successfully run blogs on GitHub Pages. After analyzing them and finding a theme I liked, I began adding a blog to my existing site. Or, to be precise, Codex began doing the work.

If you are curious about what kind of blog Codex can build on GitHub Pages, take a look at this site. It was created using only the five free hours of GPT Plus usage.

## I Still Have More Questions Than Answers

I have also learned that WordPress would be much better in many respects for a full-scale blog. But I do not need a full-scale operation yet. For the same reason, I do not plan to register a separate domain, which would cost about $20 a year, for the time being. I do not need Google Ads or other advertising either.

I still have no idea which direction these apps will take or which customers' problems and needs they will eventually solve.

Only now do I feel that I am beginning to understand business. I am learning the importance of planning, the role of marketing, and even the uncomfortable truth that a feature a developer finds interesting—or adds simply because it can be built—may be completely useless to users.

As I thought through these questions, read books, and searched for YouTubers who had gone through similar experiences, I learned that even reaching 100 app installs is genuinely difficult.

This is probably not just a problem for today's AI-assisted solo developers. It is closer to the fundamental nature of business—an area I had never needed or wanted to understand before.

In the end, if I want to run a business, I need to understand business. I still have a mountain to learn.

Even so, I feel incredibly fortunate that AI can accelerate not only development but all of these other areas as well, helping me move through trial and error much faster.
