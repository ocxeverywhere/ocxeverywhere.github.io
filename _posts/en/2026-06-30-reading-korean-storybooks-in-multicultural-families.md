As my children grow, I realize more and more how important reading time is. For multicultural families, however, it can bring an extra concern.

> “I want to read Korean storybooks to my child, but I am not confident in my pronunciation.”

Some families may have a different concern.

> “Mom is Korean, but Dad would like to read with our child too—even though he does not know Korean well.”

More families share these worries than you might expect. But now, things can be a little different.

![A parent and child reading together with English-localized speech bubbles](/assets/images/posts/lazydaddy-multicultural-korean-storybook-reading-en.png)

## Let the App Help with the Korean

Simply take a picture of a book page, and LazyDaddy reads the Korean sentences aloud naturally.

Dad can look at the illustrations and talk about the story with the child while the app handles the Korean reading. Even when family members speak different languages, they can still create meaningful reading time together.

I originally started building this app to make storybook time more fun for my own children. As I continued using it, I realized it could also be genuinely helpful for multicultural families.

Even if you do not know Korean well or feel unsure about your pronunciation, you can still spend time reading and laughing with your child.

## Do Not Let a Language Barrier Take Away Story Time

Reading is not just about sounding out words. It is a chance to hear the same story, laugh together, and imagine together with your child.

I hope a language barrier never makes a family give up that time. 😊

If you know a parent who is not a native Korean speaker or is learning Korean, please share LazyDaddy with them. It could make Korean story time with their child much easier.
