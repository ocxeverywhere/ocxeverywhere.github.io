When you are raising a child, there is one request you may hear several times a day:

> “Dad, read me a book!”

At first, reading together is a joy. But after five or ten books before bedtime, your throat starts to hurt and it takes more energy than you might expect.

That is why many parents look for different ways to help. Sound books, SayPen, audiobooks, and, more recently, AI read-aloud apps are among the most common options. Here is how they differ based on my own experience.

## 1. The Most Familiar Approach: Reading Aloud Yourself

From a child’s perspective, having a parent read aloud is probably the best option.

You can bring the story to life, pause to answer questions, and, most importantly, spend meaningful time looking at the book together.

In reality, however, it is difficult to read a large number of books every day. By bedtime in particular, many parents have almost no energy left.

## 2. Sound Books

Sound books play songs or narration at the press of a button. Children can use them independently, and they are extremely simple to operate.

There is one drawback, though. Because the audio is built into each book, reading a new story means buying another sound book. That is also why these books tend to pile up at home.

## 3. SayPen

SayPen remains one of the most widely used reading tools among parents.

Children enjoy touching a word or sentence with the pen and hearing it read aloud. It can be an especially useful option for learning with English books.

However, SayPen only works with compatible books. It often cannot read ordinary picture books you already own or books borrowed from the library.

## 4. Audiobooks

Audiobooks are convenient in the car, on the go, or before bedtime.

The experience is a little different from reading a picture book while looking at the illustrations, though. Young children may miss the fun of turning the pages and exploring the pictures together.

## 5. AI Read-Aloud Apps, a More Recent Option

Apps that use AI to read books aloud have also begun to appear. The process is simpler than you might think.

You take a picture of a book page, and the app recognizes the text and reads it in a natural voice.

LazyDaddy, which I built because I needed it myself and now use at home, works this way. The most convenient part is that it can be used with library books, old picture books at home, or virtually any other book.

I especially like that it is not tied to a particular publisher or a dedicated collection the way SayPen is. Even when my child suddenly brings over a new book, we can start reading it right away.

## The Differences I Noticed

- **Sound books:** The simplest option, but limited to their built-in content
- **SayPen:** Powerful for English learning, but requires compatible books
- **Audiobooks:** Convenient for listening on the go
- **AI read-aloud apps:** Can be used with virtually any book

There is no single correct answer. The best choice depends on your child’s age and the situation.

Our family started with sound books, tried SayPen as well, and recently began using an AI read-aloud app alongside them.

If your family visits the library often or already has many picture books at home, an AI-based option may be worth trying at least once.

If you want to read more books with your child but do not always have enough time or energy, explore these options and find the approach that works best for your family.
