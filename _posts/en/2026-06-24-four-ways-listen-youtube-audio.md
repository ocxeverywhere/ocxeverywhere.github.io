YouTube is full of genuinely useful information.

There are AI courses, developer videos, economic analysis, self-development content, and much more. But when you are commuting or driving, you cannot keep watching the screen.

While commuting or taking a walk, I often find myself thinking, “I do not need the visuals for this video. I just want to hear it.”

Here are four ways to listen to YouTube like audio on a smartphone.

## 1. Use YouTube Premium

This is the simplest option.

With YouTube Premium, you can:

- Keep playing a video after turning off the screen
- Continue playback in the background while using another app
- Watch without ads

The drawback is the monthly subscription fee.

## 2. Use Desktop Mode in a Browser

Open YouTube in Chrome and follow these steps to listen to the audio:

1. Request the desktop site.
2. Start the video.
3. Turn off the screen.
4. Press the play button in the notification panel.

However, this method may stop working or behave inconsistently when YouTube changes its policies.

## 3. Listen as If It Were a Podcast

These days, the message often matters more than the visuals.

For example, you can understand plenty of content without looking at the screen:

- AI news
- Developer courses
- Economic analysis
- Interviews

That is why many people consume content like a podcast while on the move.

## 4. Use an App That Reads Captions Aloud

Of the methods I have tried recently, this has been the most satisfying.

It extracts the captions from a YouTube video and reads them aloud with text-to-speech (TTS). In practice, I found several benefits.

### Much Lower Battery Use

The YouTube app has to keep decoding and playing video.

A caption-to-speech approach does not play the video itself, so it can greatly reduce CPU and network use. The difference is especially noticeable during a long commute or a trip.

### No Need to Look at the Screen

If the content does not depend on visuals, listening can actually make it easier to focus.

I often use this approach for AI news, developer courses, investment information, and economic trends. It feels similar to listening to an audiobook or podcast.

### Lower Mobile Data Use

Because it works mainly with text and speech instead of streaming video, it can also reduce mobile data use.

## Final Thoughts

YouTube is a treasure trove of information, but we do not always need to watch the screen.

Audio-first consumption is often more efficient while we are on the move. If you regularly watch AI news, developer courses, or interviews, I recommend trying a caption-to-speech approach.

I have recently been using an app called JustListen for this. It lets me experience YouTube videos much like podcasts, and I have been quite happy with it.

If you do not want to miss useful information while you are on the go, give it a try.
